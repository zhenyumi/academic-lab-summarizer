# Lab Profile Synthesis

## Core Rule

Every user-facing claim must trace to `evidence_refs`. Do not claim current open positions from generic join-us language alone. Mark weak or unsupported conclusions as `partial`, `unknown`, `unavailable`, or `skipped`.

## Inputs

- `lab_site_evidence.jsonl` (required): Site evidence from `lab-site-evidence-extraction`.
- `publications.curated.json` (required): Curated publications from `lab-publication-profile`.
- `publication_evidence.jsonl` (required): Publication-to-lab evidence.
- `research_theme_profile.json` (required): Research themes from confirmed and likely publications.
- Optional `lab_summary_input.json` with lab identity fields.

## Outputs

- `position_signals.json`: Extracted position signals with evidence backing.
- `lab_summary_assessment.json`: Structured lab profile evaluation.
- `lab_profile.json`: Aggregated lab profile with all evidence references.
- `report.md`: Human-readable lab profile report.
- `reports/lab-summaries/<task_id>/report.html`: Canonical human-friendly HTML report with research themes, position signals, assessment, and links to raw artifacts.
- `reports/lab-summaries/<task_id>/report.md`: Matching Markdown report.
- `reports/lab-summaries/<task_id>/report_manifest.json`: Metadata used by `reports/index.html`.
- `lab_summary_audit.json`: Quality audit with metrics, blocking, warnings.

Worker artifacts are written under `lab_summaries/<lab_id>/`. User-facing reports are generated by script under `reports/lab-summaries/<task_id>/`.

## Evidence Reference Format

This skill references both site evidence and publication evidence. Use namespaced strings:

- `site:<evidence_id>` — references a row in `lab_site_evidence.jsonl`
- `pub:<candidate_id>` — references a candidate in `publications.curated.json`

Examples: `["site:4"]`, `["pub:1", "pub:5"]`, `["site:2", "pub:3"]`

## Hard Constraints

- Do not perform new crawls or new publication searches.
- Do not claim current open positions from generic join-us language alone (e.g., "Join our team", "We are hiring" without role specifics).
- Every user-facing claim must have `evidence_refs` pointing to site evidence or publication evidence.
- Mark weak or unsupported conclusions as `partial`, `unknown`, `unavailable`, or `skipped`.
- Do not summarize ambiguous publications as lab research output.
- All synthesis artifacts MUST be produced by running `run_lab_profile_synthesis.py`. Do not generate `position_signals.json`, `lab_summary_assessment.json`, `lab_profile.json`, `report.md`, or `lab_summary_audit.json` directly without running the script first. The AI agent may enrich script output afterwards, but the script must produce the baseline.
- After generating artifacts, run `validate_lab_summary_artifacts.py <artifact_dir>`. If validation fails, fix the artifacts before proceeding.

## Lab Profile Richness

The `lab_profile.json` output must be as complete as available evidence allows:

- **Required fields** (when evidence exists): `research_summary` (1-2 sentences describing the lab's overall research direction), `pi_title`, `pi_email` (from site evidence), `pi_affiliations`.
- **Recommended fields** (when evidence exists): `department`, `lab_size` (postdocs, PhD students count), `position_signal` (explicit opening status).
- Use `lab_site_evidence.jsonl` entries with `claim_type: "pi_info"` to populate PI details.
- Use `lab_site_evidence.jsonl` entries with `claim_type: "research_direction"` to write `research_summary`.
- **Limitations** must be specific and actionable (at least 2 items), not generic one-liners. Examples: "No explicit position posting found on lab website or institutional job board.", "Publication profile limited to OpenAlex; Semantic Scholar cross-check recommended for citation metrics."
- The deterministic runner generates a minimal baseline. **The AI agent must enrich the lab profile** using all available site and publication evidence.

## Publication Overview Quality

The `publication_overview` field in `important_publications` contains structured sub-fields that the deterministic runner populates with baseline values. When the AI agent runs this skill, it should enhance empty sub-fields using available evidence:

### Sub-field Enhancement Rules

| Sub-field | Deterministic baseline | AI enhancement source |
|---|---|---|
| `one_line` | First sentence from `abstract`, or `title` if no abstract | Rewrite as a concise contribution statement (not a title rewording) using `abstract` and `match_rationale` |
| `research_question` | Empty | Extract from `abstract` — identify the question or hypothesis the paper addresses |
| `key_finding` | Second sentence from `abstract` if available, else empty | Extract or refine from `abstract`; may combine multiple sentences into one clear finding statement |
| `methods` | Empty | Extract from `abstract` — identify key experimental or computational approaches |
| `significance` | `"Relates to lab theme: {theme}"` if theme exists | Enrich using `match_rationale` and `theme` — explain how this publication connects to the lab's research direction |

### Enhancement Constraints

- Only enhance `important_publications` entries. Do not modify other sections.
- Enhanced content must be factual and traceable to `abstract`, `match_rationale`, `theme`, or `publication_evidence`. Do not fabricate findings or methods not stated in the source material.
- When no abstract is available and the agent cannot enhance from other evidence, leave sub-fields empty — the rendering layer will show `[Overview limited — no abstract available]`.
- Keep each sub-field concise: aim for one sentence each (max 250 characters).
- Do not remove or overwrite a non-empty `significance` field generated by the runner; only enrich it if you have additional context.

## Required Position Analysis

`position_signals.json` is mandatory, even when no openings are found.

Allowed `position_category` values:

- `phd`
- `masters`
- `undergraduate`
- `postdoc`
- `research_assistant`
- `technician`
- `lab_manager`
- `staff_scientist`
- `other`
- `none`

Allowed `signal_strength` values:

- `confirmed_opening`: Explicit role-specific opening with application details, deadline, start date, or clear availability.
- `likely_opening`: Role-specific recruitment language without enough details to confirm a current opening.
- `generic_recruitment`: Generic "join us" / "contact us" language without role specificity.
- `closed_or_past`: Filled, closed, or clearly past opportunity.
- `none`: No position signal found.
- `unknown`: Evidence is too unclear to classify.

Every signal must include `source_url`, `snippet`, `evidence_refs`, `confidence`, and `last_observed_or_posted_date` when available. Generic recruitment language must not be upgraded to `confirmed_opening`. **Weak/generic signals must not be reported as current open positions. They may be noted as potential interest with explicit caveats.**

## Assessment Dimensions

`lab_summary_assessment.json` must cover:

- **Research focus**: The lab's confirmed research themes and directions as evident in publications and site evidence.
- **Publication profile**: Recent publications, attribution confidence, and theme coverage.
- **Position availability**: Based on position signals, not inference.
- **Lab activity and trajectory**: PI career stage, lab size indicators, publication trajectory.
- **Methods and approaches**: Techniques and experimental approaches documented for the lab.
- **Funding indicators**: Grant mentions, lab resources, institutional support signals.

Each dimension must include `evidence_refs`, a `confidence` value (`high`, `medium`, `low`, `unknown`), a `status` (`assessed`, `partial`, `unavailable`, `skipped`), and `limitations` (array of strings).

## Workflow

1. Read all required inputs.
2. Extract and classify position signals from site evidence → `position_signals.json`.
3. Evaluate lab profile across dimensions → `lab_summary_assessment.json`.
4. Aggregate into lab profile with full evidence traceability → `lab_profile.json`.
5. Generate human-readable worker report → `report.md`.
6. Run quality audit → `lab_summary_audit.json`.
7. Generate the canonical HTML/Markdown report package by script → `reports/lab-summaries/<task_id>/report.html`, `report.md`, and `report_manifest.json`.
8. Refresh `reports/index.html` after the full workflow completes.
